ROBOT

Robotics and Optical coherence tomography for optical BiOpsy in the digestive Tract

This project proposes a disruptive approach to cancer diagnosis in the digestive tract. It deals with fundamental research, yet it is deeply inspired by the clinical needs and it mixes mathematics (compressed sensing, robot control), physics (optics) and engineering (optical MEMS, robotic endoscope, flexible continuous robot).

My work concerns the visual servoing of the robot (intracorporeal navigation) based on the 3D imaging obtained by the OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography).
In the literature, the medical imaging (such as ultrasound, MRI, confocal microscope, etc.) is used to synthesize different control laws which allow surgical tasks to be performed (e.g., biopsy, ablation, etc.). However, the OCT is not a comman sensor in the visual servo loop despite its very interesting characteristics (e.g., real-time acquisition, 3D images, high resolution, etc.). Indeed, there are some works based on the use of wavelet coefficients of OCT images (B-scan) in the control loop, but these works are limited to controlling only 3-DoF (Degrees Of Freedom) of the robot.
My postdoctoral research aims to develop control methods, going beyond the state of the art, by allowing the 6-DoF of the robot to be controlled using the volume (C-scan) obtained by the OCT as the control law input signal [1].
